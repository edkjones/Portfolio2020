Overview

Gazer is a platform created to allow cloud-based capabilities while also maintaining the dynamic of a social media app. Users have the ability to share content, including links, files, and multimedia from around the web with their network, while also having the ability to create original content.

Problem

Users typically have to choose between cloud-based apps and social media platforms due to the limitations around how content is shared. This leaves little room for personal expression and connecting on an interactive level with others.  

Solution

Gazer aims to combine the joy of sharing curated content with others and allowing a space for organizing posts into specific groups and categories. The user also has the ability to create and share original content with their network. Documents, files, links and multimedia can now be easily shared and organized.

Discovery Phase

To better understand the user, I began my research with a survey to discover the joys and pain points surrounding content sharing and cloud based applications. I attempted to ask questions to better understand the behaviors and priorities of those who use similar apps (social media/cloud-based). I suspected that the ability to upload original content, sharing easily with friends, and being able to interact with others would be the most important features to users.

  • 80% of participants frequently use both Google Drive and Instagram
  • 53% of these participants also use facebook
  • 80% frequently share content they find online with others
  • 60% frequently create orignal content that they like to share with others
  • 66% of those users state they typically use social media to share this content
When asked what they like about their favorite platform users replied:
  • “I really enjoy the simplicity of the design”  - Google Docs
  • “Connecting with friends and family” - Facebook
  • “Gives me a platform to express myself” - Instagram
When asked which features were most important when sharing content with others:
  • 80% of users stated the ability to upload a file (PDF, Video, Image, Doc
  • 73% stated the ability to easily share links and engage with other user’s posts
  • 67% wanted the ability to organize content into folders
When asked about what they currently dislike, users replied:
  • “I dislike targeted ads based off my personal data” - Facebook
  • “I hate how everything now is sponsered, unauthentic, or just an ad” - Instagram
  • “Having everything stored in different locations and forgetting where they are” - Google Docs

The results of the survey supported the theory that users desire a space to connect with others in an authentic way, while still maintaining the ability to share original content, and other things they find on the web.

Competitive Analysis

The three platforms I chose to analyze were Google Docs, Dropbox, and Pinterest. All are content sharing apps that allow users to share files with others. However, these platforms lack a key social networking aspect that would allow for more user engagement.

Google Drive is an excellent resource for users to access files anywhere on the go. They also allow a feature to share files with others. However, Google Drive lacks the ability for users to engage with others in a social setting. By adding more engaging features, Google Drive can maximize the potential for users working in teams or collaborations.

Pinterest offers a reliable way for users to group and collect visual content. However, the app is limited to images and does not give users many option to engage with others. With more UX development, Pinterest could maximize their potential by improving the ability for users to buy and sell items from visual content.

Dropbox gives users the ability to share and store files in one central location. Allowing users to showcase projects in a streamlined manner can be beneficial to those working on big teams to track progress. Users can also create original documents, but the level of interaction and engagement offered is limited.

Wireframes

My original wireframes crafted a simple design that consisted of onboarding, functionality and a simple layout design . With user testing, I was able to recognize areas of opportunity to limit the buttons and highlight essential pages.

Branding & Identity

When thinking of a name and logo design, I wanted something that would be catchy and easily recognizable to users. Essentially the app was built to encourage users to explore the web and easily share content with others. The name Gazer seemed to fit this goal, along with an decorated eye staged as the logo to promote easy brand awareness.

Visual Design

My goal in creating Gazer was to provide a comforting and simple design that was easy on the eyes, but still fun and unique. I combined complimentary colors and typography to emphasize a calm and clean product.  Museo and Open Sans were chosen as fun and easy to read typography to further the concept of simplicity and easygoingness
